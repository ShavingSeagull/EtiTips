Canada has an extensive public transport network, including bus, rail, subway, plane and ferry. Bus will be the primary method of travel to and from smaller towns, but most cities feature a subway system and there are numerous rail options for short and long distance travel. Owing to its vast size, travelling by air will often be the primary mode of travel, epecially if you're planning on visiting many places in the country.

Cars are driven on the right-hand side of the road.

Tipping

Taxis

- Generally acceptable rate: 15% of fare

As with virtually every other service in Canada, you are expected to tip your taxi driver at the end of the journey. Generally speaking, this will be around 15% of the total fare, but you can of course give a little more or a little less, depending on your service. If your driver has helped you with luggage on an airport run, it might be nice to give a little extra.

Behaviour

Standards of behaviour on Canadian public transport systems are generally good, with the mild manners of the people transferring to the way they behave on transport. Buses and trains can often get busy during peak periods, so be sure to apologise for nudging or bumping into someone. Keep your feet of the seats and keep noise to a minimum.

Be sure to give your seat up for somebody vulnerable, such as an elderly person or someone disabled. Remember, when you travel to other countries you are serving as an ambassador for your own. It will set a very good precedent if you do the right thing and aid those who need it where you can. It won't cost you anything and won't inconvenience you for more than a matter of minutes.

Do's

  • Tip your taxi driver
  • Remember your manners on public transport
  • Be prepared to travel by air if you want to see a lot of the country

Don'ts

  • Worry about the public transport network. It is safe and reliable
  • Be afraid to hire a car while holidaying in Canada. The country is vast and the distance between places can be huge
  • Plan to travel across the width of the country if you have a fear of flying...
