Totalling 50 states and 3.8 million square miles (9.8 million square kilometers), the United States is one of the world's largest countries, as well as being the wealthiest and a global superpower. Relatively young in age, the US has established itself on the world stage in a very short amount of time.

The capital city is Washington D.C and the currency is the US Dollar ($).

Behaviour

The stereotype of the loud, brash, arrogant American is alive and well for many people in Europe, who tend to be rather reserved by comparison. This is, of course, not true of every American at all and it is as easy to find a quiet reserved character as it is somebody more boistrous. That being said, social life in the United States is predicated on being outgoing and friendly. This can sometimes be seen as a surface-deep fa√ßade, especially in areas like Los Angeles, where image is everything. For the most part, however, everyday folks tend to help each other out and try their best to get along, especially in smaller towns and suburbs.

To be well received in American society, it is important to try and make an effort socially. Unlike in Europe, where small talk with strangers can be viewed with suspicion, in America it is quite commonplace. People tend to make an effort socially here, so don't be afraid to speak up! This will usually be less true in big cities, but certainly in smaller towns, you are likely to be enagaged in conversation by friendly locals.

Love

The United States is made up of people from vastly different cultures. Some of those cultures are passionate, some are reserved, so to give advice on things such as public displays of affection can be rather tricky! Generally speaking, it is better to err on the side of caution and keep those things to a minimum, though don't be surprised to see some locals fly in the face of that rule!

Same sex relationships and marriages are legal in the US, but attitude toward such things is a mixed bag. Some states are more accepting and in general this will apply to all metropolitan areas. The reverse is true in some states, however, so as with public displays of affection mentioned above, it is just generally wiser to err on the side of caution. Typically, it will be the smaller towns in more Southern states that will react negatively to same-sex relationships, so this could be something to bear in mind when planning your itinerary. Folks in those areas are often some of the friendliest, too, though.

Do's

  • Engage the locals in conversation
  • Be prepared to explain where your country is in the world
  • Try to see as much of the country as you can. It is vast with very differing areas

Don'ts

  • Be afraid to speak up. Americans are generally a rather outgoing bunch
  • Speak ill of the country while you're there, should you feel the need to. Americans are also a patriotic bunch
  • Be afraid to speak Spanish, if it's your native language. There are a large number of Latin people in America and it is possible to find large areas that speak the language, especially in Florida and the Southern states.
