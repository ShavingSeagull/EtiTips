The country of the Renaissance, passion and the world's most famous empire, Italy is truly one of the most exquisite places on Earth. Whether you are a lover of art, philosophy, wine or history, you'll find something to float your boat here. While having fallen under harder times in recent years, Italy remains one of the bastions of Europe; showcasing the very best of European culture, history and lifestyle.

The capital city is Rome and the currency is the Euro (€).

Behaviour

Greetings are a big thing in Italy. Whether meeting someone for the first time or the hundreth, you are expected to greet them when you meet and give a farewell when you part ways. Typically, this will be by using Buongiorno/Buona sera (good day/good evening), said both as the greeting and the farewell. The word Ciao is known and used throughout the world, but you shouldn't use this with strangers! Ciao is much more informal and used only for familiar faces. Also, note that when friends greet each other (when one or both are female), it is common to exchange a kiss on either cheek, starting with the left. For less-known acquaintances, a firm hand shake is the way to go.

One of the most common misconceptions for foreign tourists is that all Italians are rather hot-headed by nature. This impression is certainly understandable if you watch two Italians engage in conversation, but it isn't actually true at all. Italians are known for their fiery passion in all avenues of life, including the way they speak to each other. If a local believes you are wrong, they will tell you so and many a gentle chat have evolved into a heated debate this way. If you want to fit in with the local social life, however, you are going to have to inject a little more oomph into your social behaviour. This would be especially true of someone from the UK, for example, where the majority of native people are rather reserved by nature.

No section on Italian social behaviour would be complete without mentioning the hand gestures! Italians are famed for their passionate gesticulations and to find someone who doesn't speak with their hands here would be very rare indeed. While not a requirement to fit in with Italian social life, it certainly wouldn't hurt to let your hands loose for a little conversing of their own!

Finally, a word on English speaking: in hotels and cities, you are likely to find someone who at least speaks a little of the world's lingua franca, but don't expect to be understood by a huge number of people. This is especially true the further outside of cities you travel. Fortunately, while speaking English can be a little difficult in Italy, using your hands and big gestures can actually make you understood most of the time. So, as mentioned above, don't be afraid to let your hands do a little talking of their own if you need to be understood!

Love

Italy, along with France, is the joint-most famous country on planet Earth for romance. Many a woman has swooned at the thought of a Casanova sweeping her off her feet whilst whispering their sweet nothings and many a man has dreamed of meeting a voluptuous Latin brunette. Is there any truth to the stereotype, however? In short, yes. Though, with all the unwritten rules, the dating etiquette in Italy isn't as straight forward as you may think...

Dress to impress! Italy is home to some of the world's most prestigious fashion houses for a reason. More on the general dress sense in the Culture tab, but needless to say, first impressions count for everything in Italy and this goes for the dating scene, too. So, no turning up in a hoody and trainers! Whatever you decide to wear, make sure it's fashionable, fresh pressed and imaculate. You will absolutely be judged on your appearance.

Don't be surprised if your date is ten minutes late! Virtually everyone is late for everything all the time in Italy, so arriving 'fashionably late' is just...well...fashionably on time. Twenty minutes is even acceptable given a valid reason (such as horrendous traffic), though any longer than that and you can kiss the prospect of a second date goodbye. So, if you're a stickler for time keeping, you may have to grit your teeth and bear it!

A final note: if your date offers to pick you up or drop you home on the first date, they aren't automatically a lecher. The public transport system is notoriously unreliable and so it is often easier just to be picked up and travel to your date location together. Similarly, they won't necessarily be expecting to be invited inside when dropping you home, either. Though, for obvious reasons, that shouldn't be taken as gospel...

Useful Words

Below are some of the most common words (such as please and thank you) that can help you get by when visiting Italy. Learning a few could also help you make a good impression on the locals! Phonetic pronunciation is in parentheses ().

  • Buongiorno (bwon-jorno) - Good morning/good day/hello/general greeting/farewell.
  • Buona sera (bwonah-serah) - Good evening/general greeting. Used after the sun goes down.
  • Ciao (chow) - Hi/Bye. More informal that the above greetings and shouldn't be used with strangers. Note that it is used for a greeting and a farewell.
  • Grazie mille (gratzee-meeleh) - Thank you very much.
  • Grazie (gratzee) - Thanks. More informal than Grazie Mille, but acceptable in shops etc.
  • Per favore (per-fah-voreh) - Please.
  • Prego (prehgo) - You're welcome.
  • Arrivederci (ah-reeveh-der-chi) - Good bye. Usuable all day.
  • Addio (ad-dio) - Farewell
  • Sì (see) - Yes.
  • No (no) - No. Yup, just like in English.
  • Mi dispiace (mee-dis-pee-ahcheh) - Sorry.
  • Mi scusi (mee-scoo-see) - Excuse me. Useful when you want to pass by someone on the street.

Do's

  • Be sure to properly greet and farewell! Buongiorno is your friend
  • Speak with your hands if you are able to. It will certainly help you to be understood
  • Make use of the common words above! English isn't widely spoken in Italy

Don'ts

  • Be abashed by passionate speaking. Give as good as you get. The locals will think you're strange if you don't
  • Rely on English to get you by outside of big cities. Hand gestures are your friend!
  • Dress like a hermit if you are dating in Italy. You will get nowhere in record time
