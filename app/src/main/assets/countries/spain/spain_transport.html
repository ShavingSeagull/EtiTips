Spain boasts an impressive public transport network, with trains, a variety of bus systems (both short and long distance available) and even trams in a few areas. Large cities, such as Madrid, Barcelona, Bilbao and Valencia also boast a metro system, which can be useful in avoiding the typically heavy traffic of a metropolitan area.

Cars are driven on the right-hand side of the road.

Tipping

Taxis

- No tipping expected

As with much of the rest of Spain, tipping taxi drivers is by no means an expected custom. You would be hard-pressed to find a local tipping a driver, so it's safe to follow suit. Of course, if the driver has helped you with mountains of luggage or rushed frantically so you wouldn't miss your flight home, leaving a little something certainly wouldn't be turned down!

Behaviour

Like the rest of the country, people are generally well behaved on public transport in Spain. Taking phone calls is permitted on transport, but it is best to keep your voice down as much as you can; if you find people glaring at you, you have probably been too loud. Contrary to expectation for such a hot country, not all buses have air conditioning in Spain and in the summer during busy periods, it can get very uncomfortable. Think of your fellow passengers and make sure you wear deodorant...

Be sure to offer your seat to vulnerable people, such as the elderly or disabled, especially on city buses as there is minimal seating to make way for standing passengers. Do the right thing and give a helping hand to those in need. You are representing your country when you go abroad, so making the effort to act courteously will leave a positive impression on the locals.

Do's

  • Speak with your indoor voice on public transport
  • Move inside the vehicle as soon as you board. It's best not to clog the door area
  • Give up your seat for somebody vulnerable

Don'ts

  • Raise your voice on public transport
  • Tip your taxi driver unless he has proven to be a superhero
  • Be afraid to indulge in the local transport network. While the buses can sometimes be confusing, the trains and metro can be a great way to get around the cities and country at large
