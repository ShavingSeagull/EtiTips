New Zealand boasts a good public transport network, with train, bus, ferry and domestic flights being options, as well as the usual taxis. Owing to the nation's low population density, using a car is advised, especially if travelling to rural areas as there will often be no other option to get around.

Cars are driven on the left-hand side of the road.

Tipping

Taxis

- No tipping expected

Tipping taxis follows suit with much of the rest of New Zealand in that it isn't really expected. Taxi drivers tend to earn fairly well, making tipping redundant. You will sometimes hear people say "Keep the change", but this is out of a desire not to carry around pockets of coins as opposed to wanting to leave a tip for the service. Feel free to follow suit or just don't leave anything extra at all.

Behaviour

Standards of behaviour are fairly good on Kiwi transport. The services are safe and well maintained, so it would be best to ensure you help that trend along in your behaviour. If you happened to encounter any issues while travelling, you'd likely find one or several locals stepping in to help anyway (though don't take that for granted, naturally). You also shouldn't be surprised if nearby passengers strike up a conversation, as the locals like to be sociable. Don't shy away from this, as it can be a great way to get to know the local people and the area in general.

Be sure to offer your seat to somebody vulnerable, such as someone elderly or disabled. Good manners will be appreciated here and you are representing your country when you go abroad, so it is always a good idea to leave as good an impression as you can. Just think of it this way - would you appreciate someone at home being kind enough to offer their seat to your elderly grandmother/mother?

Do's

  • Make use of a car, especially if you plan to travel to more remote areas
  • Be considerate of others around you on public transport
  • Give up your seat for somebody vulnerable

Don'ts

  • Tip taxi drivers or if you do, just let them keep the change
  • Be afraid to engage in conversation with the locals around you
  • Let a fear of foreign public transport stop you. New Zealand has lots of little islands that you won't be able to see otherwise!
