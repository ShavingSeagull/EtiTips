Mexico boasts a surprisingly wide public transport network. Complete with multiple forms of buses, rail and even a reliable metro system in Mexico City, you will often find a method of travel that will prevent the urge to jump in a taxi. Note, that in places like Mexico City, the transport networks obviously get very crowded and have seen better days. Don't be put-off by this, however, as public transport in Mexico is some of the cheapest you will ever come across.

Cars are driven on the right-hand side of the road.

Tipping

Taxis

- No tipping expected

Though tipping has become commonplace in Mexico these days, this doesn't really apply to taxis. You are free to offer a small amount (such as rounding up the bill) if your driver has helped with luggage etc, but typically you can safely forego tipping altogether. This is, of course, entirely down to your discretion.

Behaviour

Like the rest of the country, people are generally well behaved on public transport in Mexico. Taking phone calls is permitted on transport, but it is best to keep your voice down as much as you can. Contrary to expectation for such a hot country, not all buses have air conditioning in Mexico and in the summer during busy periods, it can get very uncomfortable. Think of your fellow passengers and make sure you wear deodorant...

Be sure to offer your seat to vulnerable people, such as the elderly or disabled, especially on city buses or the metro, as seating will be at a premium dueing busy periods. Do the right thing and give a helping hand to those in need. You are representing your country when you go abroad, so making the effort to act courteously will leave a positive impression on the locals. As we mentioned on the Social tab, Mexicans like foreigners, but only those who show a bit of culture and refined behaviour...

Do's

  • Make use of the metro in Mexico City. It has more than enough stops to take you where you need to go
  • Move inside the vehicle as soon as you board. It's best not to clog the door area
  • Give up your seat for somebody vulnerable

Don'ts

  • Raise your voice on public transport
  • Tip your taxi driver unless he has proven to be a superhero
  • Be afraid to indulge in the local transport network. It is cheap, comes in many different forms and is well used by the locals
