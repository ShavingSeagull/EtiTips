Australia boasts a good public transport network, with train, bus, ferry and tram being options depending on the city you are in. The local transport is safe and reliable, with strict regulations and guards at every station and on every train. There are smart card options for convenient and cheaper travel in most major cities.

Cars are driven on the left-hand side of the road.

Tipping

Taxis

- No tipping expected

Tipping taxis follows suit with much of the rest of Australia in that it isn't really expected. Taxi drivers tend to earn fairly well, making tipping redundant. You will sometimes hear people say "Keep the change", but this is out of a desire not to carry around pockets of coins as opposed to wanting to leave a tip for the service. Feel free to follow suit or just don't leave anything extra at all.

Behaviour

Standards of behaviour are fairly good on Australian transport. The services are safe and well maintained, so it would be best to ensure you help that trend along in your behaviour. If you happened to encounter any issues while travelling, you'd likely find one or several locals stepping in to help anyway (though don't take that for granted, naturally). You should also expect nearby passengers to strike up a conversation, as the locals are very sociable. Don't shy away from this, as it can be a great way to get to know the local people and the area in general.

Be sure to offer your seat to somebody vulnerable, such as someone elderly or disabled. Good manners will be appreciated here and you are representing your country when you go abroad, so it is always a good idea to leave as good an impression as you can. Just think of it this way - would you appreciate someone at home being kind enough to offer their seat to your elderly grandmother/mother?

Bonus non-etiquette tip! - As mentioned above, the major cities employ a smart card system for travel on public transport. This proves to be more convenient and cheaper than traditional tickets. These pay-as-you-go cards can be purchased from any station, though each city uses their own version, so you can't use Sydney's card in Melbourne, for instance. Below is a reference of the cards used in each city:

  • Sydney - Opal Card (trains, trams, buses and ferries)
  • Melbourne - myki Card (trains, trams and buses)
  • Brisbane - Go Card (trains, trams, buses and ferries)
  • Perth - SmartRider (trains, buses and ferries)
  • Canberra - MyWay (buses)
  • Adelaide - Metrocard (trains, trams and buses)
  • Hobart - Greencard (buses)
  • Darwin - Tap & Ride Card (buses)

Do's

  • Pick up a smart card for travel in the big cities
  • Be considerate of others around you on public transport
  • Give up your seat for somebody vulnerable

Don'ts

  • Tip taxi drivers or if you do, just let them keep the change
  • Be afraid to engage in conversation with the locals around you
  • Forget to wear deodorant on transport. It can get rather hot in Australia...
