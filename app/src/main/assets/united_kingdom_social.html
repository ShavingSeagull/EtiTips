Comprised of England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland, the United Kingdom is an island situated on the western cusp of Europe, just an hour's ferry ride from the continent. Perhaps best known for its long, rich history and internationally renowned monarch, Queen Elizabeth II. The UK has had a colourful history; at its peak, the British Empire covered two thirds of the globe.

Behaviour

The stereotype of the British 'stiff upper lip' has faded somewhat over time, but a certain way of conducting oneself is still expected in the UK, especially by the older population. British people generally find brashness and arrogance to be a turn-off, while a loud boisterous display will often be met with tuts by passers-by.

To be well received in British society - especially outside the big cities - you should be polite, remember to say please and thank you, show courtesy to older people and keep an air of civility during times when you may be tempted to let it go.

Love

Same sex relationships and marriages are legal in the UK and are on the whole tolerated by the native population. If you are concerned about your sexuality when travelling abroad, you will have no fears in the UK.
