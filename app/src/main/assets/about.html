Current version - v1.0

EtiTips is an app for aiding your travels abroad, by giving you helpful tips and advice on etiquette in the areas of social behaviour, food and drink, general culture and public transport. It can often be the case, for example, that while dining at a restaurant, the thought suddenly occurs that you aren't sure if it is customary to tip the waiter in the country you're visiting or not. In some places it's expected, in others it's considered rude!

Luckily, EtiTips is at hand to save your blushes!

What if I don't have internet access abroad?

No problem! Unlike most other apps on the market today, EtiTips doesn't require internet access to function; it is an entirely offline app.

We thought it would be silly to create a service that requires internet access when you're abroad, seeing as it isn't necessarily the case that you'll have access to the net - WiFi can be spotty and data roaming charges can be painful! It would be a huge disappointment to find yourself in need of a quick tip, only to discover you don't have internet access. So, the decision to make EtiTips offline was made.

I'm from country x and these tips aren't true!

Please note that the advice given in EtiTips is intended for foreign visitors to your country. You may take some of the things you see here for granted because you live in the country you're reading about, but for a visitor it can be easy to make mistakes. It is often the silly little things that can be missed, but is often those that can have the most impact on the native population. The tips and advice in this app are designed to give foreign visitors the best chance of conducting themselves in a way that is both respectful and natural to the native people and culture.

Are there plans to expand EtiTips?

Certainly! The more countries we can feature, the more people are able to make use of the service. There are also plans to add translations in other languages, as this would make the benefits of EtiTips available to non-English speaking people, too. We have plans for more features in the pipeline as well, so stay tuned.

Is there an iOS version?

Not at this time. It isn't in the plans to launch an iOS version of EtiTips, but this could potentially change in the future.
